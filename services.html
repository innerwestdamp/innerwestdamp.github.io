Rising Damp

In simple terms rising damp occurs when ground water travels upwards through porous building materials such as brick, sandstone, or mortar.

This dampness then extends to cause secondary dampness to a building resulting in problems such as paint flaking, wall stains, and the appearance of mould. Watch the video below for a more complete explanation.
